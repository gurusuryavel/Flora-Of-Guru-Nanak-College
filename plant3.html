Naringi crenulata (Roxb.) D.H. Nicolson

Classification

Order: Fabales

Family: Rutaceae Juss.

Species: Naringi crenulata (Roxb.) D.H. Nicolson

Etymology

The term Naringi is derived from the Indian word 'Narinja' which means the strong smell; the specific epithet is derived from the Latin word 'crenulata' means leaf with crenulate margin.

Distribution

Native to India; Distributed in Indo-Malaysia.

Regional Names

  • Tamil - Magaavilvam
  • Telugu - Kukka velaga
  • Hindi - Beli
  • English - Toothed-leaf Limonia

Description

  • Habit:A small evergreen tree grows up to 5 m tall.
  • Leaves:Compound, up to 15 cm long; leaflets 5-7, opposite, sessile, crenulate or minutely toothed.
  • Flower: Umbel, white, pedicellate, calyx 4, free or united at base, petals 4, free, elliptic or oblong, glandular.
  • Fruit: Berry.
  • Seeds: 1-4, dull yellow, smooth.

Phenology

Flowering - March to September; Fruiting- April to December.

What is Carbon Sequestering ?

Carbon sequestering, also known as carbon capture and storage (CCS) or carbon sequestration, is a process by which carbon dioxide (CO2) is captured from the atmosphere or from industrial sources, such as power plants, and stored in long-term reservoirs to prevent it from entering the atmosphere and contributing to climate change.

Uses

  1. Bark decoction is used for boils.
  2. Roots paste is applied for rheumatism, and root bark paste is applied for body pain.
  3. Leaves consumed for ulcer.
  4. Bark, leaves, and roots are ground into paste given orally to increase fertility in women.

Medicinal Properties

  1. Bark decoction is made into a paste and applied on boils.
  2. Roots crushed with onion, garlic, and calcium made into a paste applied for rheumatism
  3. The Root bark is made into a paste and applied in body pain
  4. Powdered roots are purgative and stomachic.
  5. Leaves are soaked in buttermilk and consumed to get relief from ulcers.
